About Accounting Package

Why Learn Tally Accounting?

Tally is widely used by businesses, shops, offices, accounting firms, and finance teams for recording transactions, preparing bills, managing inventory, and generating financial reports.

Practical Accounting Skill

Learn real accounting entries, vouchers, ledgers, invoices, and financial records.

Job-Oriented Training

Useful for accounting assistants, office staff, finance learners, and business owners.

Billing & Inventory

Create sales bills, purchase entries, stock records, inventory reports, and payment records.

Financial Reporting

Prepare profit and loss, balance sheet, cash book, ledger reports, and trial balance.

Tally Course Modules

What You Will Learn

This course focuses on practical accounting workflows and Tally software skills required for business, office, and accounting environments.

Company Creation

Create company profiles, set financial year, configure accounting features, and manage company information in Tally.

Ledgers & Groups

Create ledgers, groups, capital accounts, cash/bank accounts, debtors, creditors, expenses, and income accounts.

Voucher Entry

Learn payment, receipt, contra, journal, sales, purchase, debit note, and credit note voucher entries.

Billing & Invoicing

Create sales invoices, purchase bills, customer bills, supplier invoices, and billing records.

Inventory Management

Manage stock items, stock groups, units, purchase stock, sales stock, and inventory reports.

Financial Reports

Generate trial balance, profit and loss account, balance sheet, ledger reports, cash book, and day book.
Practical Accounting Tasks

Practice Real Office Accounting Work

Students will practice real accounting tasks commonly used in offices, shops, small businesses, finance departments, and accounting firms.

Cash & Bank Entries

Record receipts, payments, deposits, withdrawals, bank transactions, and cash book entries.

Purchase & Sales Entries

Record purchase bills, sales invoices, returns, supplier accounts, and customer transactions.

Debtors & Creditors

Manage customer balances, supplier balances, outstanding reports, and payment follow-up records.

Stock Records

Maintain stock opening balance, stock movement, stock summary, inventory valuation, and product records.

Tax Concepts

Understand basic VAT/GST concepts, tax invoices, tax entries, and compliance-related record keeping.

Printing Reports

Print invoices, ledgers, statements, receipts, payment vouchers, and financial reports.
Learning Pathway

Step-by-Step Tally Learning Process

01

Understand Basic Accounting

Learn accounting terms, debit and credit, assets, liabilities, income, expenses, capital, and transaction concepts.

02

Set Up Company in Tally

Create a company, set financial year, configure accounting options, and understand Tally interface.

03

Create Ledgers and Groups

Prepare account groups, ledgers, party accounts, cash/bank accounts, expenses, and income accounts.

04

Enter Vouchers and Bills

Practice sales, purchase, receipt, payment, contra, journal, and adjustment entries.

05

Manage Inventory and Tax

Create stock items, manage inventory movement, understand tax invoices, and record tax-related entries.

06

Generate Reports

Prepare trial balance, profit and loss, balance sheet, ledger statements, stock summary, and final reports.
